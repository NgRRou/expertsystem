### Part 2: Interview Questions and Answers

**Q1: What is the main purpose of the student welfare screening process at FSKTM?**
**A:** Our primary goal is to ensure that students who are truly in need—especially those from B40 backgrounds or facing sudden disasters—receive the right support. However, we cannot rely solely on the MAYA portal or student database because many students don't update their info. We need an advisory system that guides them through the official application process.

**Q2: Who is eligible for this screening?**
**A:** Only active students in our Diploma or Degree programmes. Masters or PhD students usually have different funding channels. If they aren't active, they can't apply for these specific faculty welfare funds.

**Q3: How do you categorize the financial aid for B40 students?**
**A:** For local B40 students, we have standard packages: Food coupons (RM150/month) and Internet aid (RM30/month). For "Special Cases," we can provide up to RM500, but they must have complete supporting documents. If documents are missing, we can't process the aid.

**Q4: What about M40 or T20 students? Do they get anything?**
**A:** Standard welfare funds are prioritized for B40. M40 cases are referred to the TD HEP office for manual review. T20 students are generally low priority unless they have a serious emergency or disaster case.

**Q5: Speaking of disasters, what does the Student Disaster Relief Fund cover?**
**A:** This fund is for both local and international students. It covers deaths (student or immediate family like parents/guardians/children), serious accidents, and natural disasters like floods or fires. We provide RM500 for student death, RM250 for immediate family death, and RM200 for accidents or property loss.

**Q6: What documentation is required for these claims?**
**A:** It's critical. For death cases, we need the death certificate. For accidents, we need police and medical reports. For fires or floods, we need reports from authorities and photographic proof. If they don't have these, we request them before proceeding.

**Q7: How do you handle scholarships?**
**A:** FSKTM doesn't "grant" scholarships directly. We act as a referral point. We look at their CGPA (usually 3.0+) and income. We tell them to check our Instagram or Gmail announcements and apply through the sponsor's channel.

**Q8: What about non-financial support like counseling?**
**A:** If a student reports stress or emotional issues, we refer them to the counseling unit. If it's affecting their academic performance, it's a High Priority referral. We also see many students with accommodation issues; we refer them to the relevant office, but if they are B40, we also check if they need financial aid for that.

**Q9: How do you prioritize these cases?**
**A:** High priority is for emergencies, disasters, and food needs. Medium is for internet aid, M40 reviews, and standard counseling. Low is for students who already have support or don't meet the welfare criteria.

**Q10: What happens if a student satisfies multiple conditions?**
**A:** We combine the recommendations. A student might need food aid AND counseling. In such cases, we assign the highest applicable priority and give them a list of all next steps.
